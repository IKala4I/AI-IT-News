### FERC Orders All Six US Grid Operators to Fast-Track AI Data Center Power

On June 18, the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission issued show-cause orders to all six US regional grid operators under its jurisdiction — the first time FERC has acted simultaneously across all of them. Each operator has 60 days to either justify existing rules or propose new tariffs across five areas: faster interconnection processing, cost-transparency safeguards, co-location rules for behind-the-meter generation, flexible large-load services, and new study processes for nearby generation. FERC Chair Laura Swett called AI data center power access "the biggest priority our country is facing at the moment." The orders cover infrastructure serving roughly 200 million Americans. The action directly fulfills a 2025 request from DOE Secretary Chris Wright to treat AI data center interconnection as a national priority.

**Why it matters:** Power availability, not GPU supply, is now the primary bottleneck for building AI infrastructure in the US. FERC's action puts regulatory weight behind what has been an informal priority. Expect grid operators to accelerate data center hookup timelines, which in turn affects where the next generation of AI data centers get built — and which states attract that investment.

## 💻 IT / General Tech News

### Your ATProto Identity Probably Isn't Yours — A Deep Dive Trending on Hacker News

Developer Kevin Alden published a detailed analysis of a fundamental tension in the AT Protocol (the standard underpinning Bluesky): despite the protocol's decentralized branding, the vast majority of users' cryptographic signing keys are controlled by their Personal Data Server (PDS) operators — not by the users themselves. The PDS signing key signs every commit to a user's repository: every post, follow, and like. A compromised or malicious PDS operator could impersonate any hosted user across the entire ATProto ecosystem, not just Bluesky, and could permanently lock users out of their identities. Alden argues that default account creation should require enrolling a backup rotation key that the user controls — the feature exists in the protocol but isn't surfaced during onboarding.

**Why it matters:** Bluesky has positioned itself as the decentralized, user-sovereign alternative to X/Twitter. If key custody defaults to operator control, that sovereignty is mostly theoretical for the average user. This is not a protocol flaw per se — the tooling exists — but an onboarding and product decision that materially changes the security model.
