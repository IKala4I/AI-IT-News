### OpenAI's New Biology Benchmark Shows How Far AI Still Is From Real Research

OpenAI released GeneBench-Pro, a 129-problem benchmark testing whether models can do research-grade computational biology reasoning (genomics, quantitative biology, translational medicine) rather than simple fact recall. Results were humbling: OpenAI's own GPT-5.6 Sol topped out at a 31.5% pass rate, while Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.8 scored just 16%. Problems were validated with outside PhDs and postdocs, and 10 sample tasks were open-sourced on Hugging Face.

**Why it matters:** It's a useful reality check against the "AI is about to accelerate scientific discovery" narrative that's been circulating this week (see NVIDIA BioNeMo and Anthropic Claude Science from yesterday) — the gap between demo-ready chat assistance and genuine research capability is still large.

Source: [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/introducing-genebench-pro/)

### arXiv Becomes an Independent Nonprofit After 25 Years at Cornell

Effective July 1, the preprint repository arXiv (3.08 million+ cumulative submissions) formally separated from Cornell University, backed by $10 million from the Simons Foundation and Schmidt Sciences to fund cloud migration and platform modernization. Headquarters remain at Cornell Tech.

**Why it matters:** As AI-paper volume keeps exploding, the infrastructure researchers rely on to share work quickly needed a more sustainable, independently-funded footing — worth knowing if your workflow depends on arXiv access or its API.

Source: [arXiv blog](https://blog.arxiv.org/2026/06/30/arxivs-next-chapter/)

### Sam Altman Pitches a Global AI Safety Forum — and Offers Washington a 5% Stake in OpenAI

In a Financial Times op-ed, Sam Altman called for a "U.S.-led international forum" modeled on the IAEA, aviation-safety bodies, and global financial standards — one that would set accepted AI safety standards, provide independent technical risk analysis, and make advanced AI available to nations and companies that follow the rules. Separately, the FT reported OpenAI has proposed giving the US government a 5% ownership stake — worth roughly $42.6 billion at its ~$852B March valuation — through a government-seeded "Public Wealth Fund," which Altman frames as a way to share AI's economic upside with the public.

**Why it matters:** It's Altman's most concrete public pitch yet for an international AI governance body with the US (and OpenAI) as standard-setter, paired with a striking move to pre-empt regulatory friction by aligning financially with the Trump administration — a template other labs may feel pressure to match.

Source: [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/07/02/sam-altman-new-world-order-ai-openai-google-anthropic/)

### Meta Launches "Meta Compute" to Sell Excess AI Infrastructure

### Godot Game Engine Bans AI-Authored Code Contributions

The open-source Godot Engine foundation announced it will require human-authored code, prohibit autonomous AI-agent pull requests and "vibe coded" submissions, and reject AI-generated text in contributor discussions — citing reviewer burnout and an inability to trust AI-heavy contributors to understand and fix their own submitted code.

**Why it matters:** It's a notable, concrete open-source governance stance pushing back against AI-generated contributions, and may become a reference point for other maintainer communities wrestling with the same reviewer-load problem.
