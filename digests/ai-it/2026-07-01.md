### Anthropic Launches Claude Sonnet 5 as the New Default Model

Anthropic released Claude Sonnet 5 on June 30, calling it its most agentic Sonnet model yet — approaching Opus 4.8-level performance on coding and autonomous tool-use tasks at a fraction of the cost. It's now the default model for Free and Pro users, with introductory pricing of $2/$10 per million input/output tokens through August 31 (rising to $3/$15 after). Simon Willison's technical review flagged a subtle detail: the new tokenizer makes English input ~1.4x more expensive per token and Spanish ~1.33x more, but leaves Simplified Mandarin roughly unchanged — a quiet pricing shift that changes real-world API costs depending on which languages an app processes.

**Why it matters:** Anthropic is closing the price/performance gap between its mid-tier and flagship models, putting high-end agentic capability within reach of Free/Pro users just as OpenAI pushes its own GPT-5.6 tier — and the tokenizer change is a reminder that "same price, new model" claims are worth checking against your actual traffic mix.

### US Lifts Export Controls on Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 After Safety Fix

The Trump administration's Commerce Department had abruptly banned foreign nationals — including non-citizen Anthropic employees — from accessing Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 over national-security concerns tied to a jailbreak technique, prompting Anthropic to disable both models for all customers. After working with the government on a new safeguard that blocks the jailbreak roughly 99% of the time, restrictions were lifted the evening of June 30, with global access fully restored July 1.

**Why it matters:** This is the first real-world case of a frontier US lab having model access nationally restricted, then restored via a negotiated technical fix rather than a policy reversal — likely a preview of how government oversight of frontier models will play out going forward.

Sources: [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/30/anthropic-says-trump-admin-has-lifted-export-controls-on-claude-fable-5-and-mythos-5.html) · [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/siladityaray/2026/07/01/trump-administration-lifts-export-controls-on-anthropics-mythos-5-and-fable-5-ai-models/)

### Subquadratic Publishes Independently Benchmarked 12M-Token Context Model

Startup Subquadratic released a technical report for SubQ 1.1 Small, a sparse-attention model claiming a 12-million-token context window using roughly 64x fewer FLOPs than dense attention at the 1M-token mark, with 100% exact-match retrieval up to 2M tokens and 89.7% pass@4 on LiveCodeBench. Notably, the benchmarks were run by third-party evaluator Appen rather than self-reported.

**Why it matters:** Long-context efficiency has been a persistent bottleneck for practical agentic workflows (large codebases, long documents), and independent verification — rare in a field full of self-reported benchmarks — makes this one worth actually watching rather than dismissing as marketing.

Source: [Subquadratic technical report](https://subq.ai/subq-1-1-small-technical-report)

## 💻 IT / General Tech News

### OAuth Supply-Chain Breach at Klue Now Confirmed Across 24+ Companies

An attacker compromised competitive-intelligence vendor Klue via an old, unused test credential, stole OAuth tokens, and used them to pull Salesforce CRM data across customer environments. New victims confirmed this week include LastPass, 8x8, and Pendo, joining an already long list — HackerOne, Snyk, Tanium, Recorded Future, OneTrust, Jamf, and Deel among them.

**Why it matters:** Another OAuth-token supply-chain attack in the mold of last year's Salesloft Drift breach — a reminder that SaaS integration tokens remain a systemic weak point even for security vendors themselves. If your org uses any of the affected tools, check for related advisories.

Sources: [SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/more-klue-breach-victims-identified-as-hackers-get-hacked/) · [BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/klue-oauth-breach-linked-to-icarus-salesforce-data-theft-attacks/)
