### Anthropic Restores Claude Fable 5 and Proposes an Industry-Wide "Jailbreak Severity" Scale

Anthropic restored global access to Claude Fable 5 on July 1 after a 2.5-week suspension triggered when the US Commerce Department imposed export controls following a reported jailbreak that got the model to produce exploit-writing guidance. The next day, Anthropic published a new four-tier cybersecurity-use classifier (Prohibited / High-Risk Dual-Use / Low-Risk Dual-Use / Benign) plus a proposed 0–4 "Cyber Jailbreak Severity" scale meant to give the industry a shared, quantitative way to describe how bad a given jailbreak actually is. It also opened a HackerOne bounty for new Fable 5 jailbreaks. The more capable, security-research-focused Mythos 5 remains locked down to roughly 100 vetted US critical-infrastructure organizations.

**Why it matters:** It's the first real attempt at a standardized vocabulary for jailbreak severity — useful for regulators and rival labs trying to compare incidents apples-to-apples — and it resolves a rare case of a US government agency forcing a frontier model offline.

### OpenAI Confirms GPT-5.6 Sol Will Run on Cerebras Chips at 750 Tokens/Second

OpenAI confirmed on July 1 that GPT-5.6 Sol — previewed in late June under limited, government-reviewed access — will launch this month on Cerebras's wafer-scale hardware at up to 750 tokens/second, roughly 6–15x faster than typical GPU-cluster inference for a frontier model. Access starts limited to select customers as Cerebras scales up capacity, building on their existing multi-year, $20B+ inference deal.

**Why it matters:** Generation speed, not just model quality, is often the real bottleneck for real-time agentic and voice applications — this could unlock use cases that feel too laggy today.

### F-Droid Blasts Google's New Android Developer Verification as "Threat Masquerading as Protection" (Top HN Story)

Google's Android Developer Verification program requires every Android developer to register centrally with government ID and pay fees, enforced by a non-removable, root-level background service on Android 8+. F-Droid argues Google's terms give it no formal definition of "malware," meaning Google alone decides what's allowed — a power F-Droid worries could later be turned against tools like ad-blockers or independent app stores. The program starts rolling out September 30 in Brazil, Indonesia, Singapore, and Thailand, potentially blocking unverified apps (including F-Droid's) globally after that.

**Why it matters:** It effectively ends Android's 18-year tradition of open sideloading, handing Google unilateral gatekeeping power over what software can run on Android — a major shift for independent developers and open-source app distribution.

Sources: [F-Droid](https://f-droid.org/2026/07/01/adv-malware.html) · [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48762725)

### Linux Disclosure: LUKS Suspend Has Silently Stopped Wiping Disk-Encryption Keys From RAM Since Kernel 6.9

A researcher found that since Linux kernel 6.9, the LUKS suspend-to-RAM path stopped clearing disk-encryption keys from memory the way it used to — a regression, not an intentional change. That means a laptop seized while merely suspended (not shut down) may be vulnerable to cold-boot key extraction.

**Why it matters:** Anyone relying on suspend/standby rather than full shutdown for laptop security should know their encryption keys may currently be recoverable from memory if the device is seized; a fix is reportedly in progress.

Sources: [Original report](https://mathstodon.xyz/@iblech/116769502749142438) · [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48763035)
